Biography

Ariana Grande

Ariana Grande

Ariana Grande is perhaps the quintessential pop star of the last half of the 2010s. "Problem," a 2014 smash duet with Iggy Azalea, was the first indication of her development, an evolution underscored by the hits "Bang Bang" and "Love Me Harder," which featured Jessie J & Nicki Minaj and the Weeknd, respectively.

Grande maintained her popularity with 2016's Dangerous Woman, then really hit her stride with 2018's Sweetener and its swift sequel Thank U, Next. That achievement was quickly equaled by "7 Rings," as well as "Positions,".

Awards

Ariana Grande first broke into the Top 10 with "The Way," from her chart-topping debut album, Yours Truly, in 2013.

Grande received her first career GRAMMY nominations for 2014 for Best Pop Duo/Group Performance for "Bang Bang" with Jessie J and Nicki Minaj and Best Pop Vocal Album for her second studio album, My Everything. Grande made her GRAMMY stage debut at the 57th GRAMMY Awards, performing "Just A Little Bit Of Your Heart."
